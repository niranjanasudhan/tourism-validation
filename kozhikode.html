Kozhikode

The largest city of Kerala, Calicut is currently known as Kozhikode. Founded in the year 1948 by Vasco Da Gama, it lies on the south west coast of Arabian Sea. Largely popular for its alluring charm and picturesque views, Calicut is indeed one of the most prominent tourist attractions of Kerala. If you read through the history of Calicut, you’ll know that the place has been under the rule of several dynasties. It has always been an opulent trading town and is an erstwhile capital of the daunting Zamorin dynasty. Another significant fact about Calicut is that its name comes from Calico which is a hand woven cloth. Calicut indeed has innumerable things to attract tourists from across the world. From nature lovers to adventure seekers, kids to the elderly, the city brings forth a myriad of things for all. The best time to make a visit to Calicut is during the months of September to March. During this time, the weather is largely pleasant and several adventure tour packages can be availed to keep you engaged and entertained. For tourists, Calicut is mainly like a hopping off point for Wayanad or for a long exploration over the Ghats leading you to Bangalore or Mysore.

Places to Visit in Kozhikode

Kozhikode Beach

Located on the Malabar coast of India, Kozhikode Beach or Calicut Beach is one of the top attractions of Calicut. This beautiful beach is visited by tourists and locals alike to watch the bewildering sunset. Enjoy a stroll at the beach or sit back and enjoy watching the sun setting into the ocean at the distance.

Kozhikode Backwaters

If you are in Calicut or Kozhikode, and do not explore the enticing backwaters there, then would you have really visited the city? We guess not. Kozhikode Backwaters are enchanting and also one of the best places to explore Kerala backwaters.

Beypore Beach

Visiting a coastal city, one can expect some beautiful beaches to explore there. Another enticing beach here away from the city rush is Beypore beach. It is a beautiful place to spend some quality time with your loved ones.

Lion’s Park

If one is looking for things to do in Calicut with kids, then the Lion’s Park is a great place to check out. Set close to the lighthouse on theKozhikode beach, this is a great beachfront park and has a nice view.

SM Street

If you are in Calicut and you do not try the sweets at SM Street, then you are missing out on the sweet bliss that this coastal city has to offer. Famously known as Mittai Theruvu, Sweetmeat Street is one of those places in Calicut which deserves a visit.

Mananchira Square

Mananchira Square is a park which surround Mananchira, a man made freshwater pond. The pond receives water from a hot water spring, and this complex which surrounds it is Mananchira Square. It acts like a public park today, and one can head here on a breezy evening during their trip to Calicut.

Pazhassi Raja Museum

If one is an art fanatic, then Pazhassiraja Museum should definitely be on their list. This is an interesting museum and art gallery in Calicut which deserves a visit on the trip to this city. It is a great place for those intrigued by history as the museum houses an amazing collection of historical artifacts.

Mishkal Mosque

The coastal town of Calicut has some really old buildings which have a unique architectural style. One such attraction is the Mishkal Mosque and if old buildings and structures intrigue you, then this is one place that can be on your Calicut list. Marvel at this mosque, which is a medieval structure on your Calicut tour.
