Malappuram

Malappuram is a northern district in Kerala known for its rich culture, historic significance and remarkable heritage. The region of Malappuram, speckled with small hills and mounds, justifies its name which in Malayalam means ‘Hill Top’. With a history that weaves ancient and modern times together, the region contributed extensively to the culture, religion, economy as well as tourism of Kerala. The district invited the attention of economists from all over the country in recent times due to its Gulf migrant population which gave rise to an unprecedented economic boom. Three rivers, namely Chaliyar, Bharathapuzha and Kadalundi, flow through the land of Malappuram enriching both its soil and its culture. The powerful army of the Zamorins kings of Calicut was headquartered here in the olden days. The name of the district is marked in the history of Indian nationalism for being the venue for Khilafat Movement and Mopila Revolt. Malappuram is the birth place of the Muslim dance form, Oppana.

Places to Visit in Malappuram

Teak Museum

The Teak Museum is located in Nilambur, Malappuram. The museum is a two-storey building and is the worlds first teak museum and is operated by the Kerala Forest Research Department. The museum also consists of a variety of articles and facts regarding the teak trees.

Adyanpara Falls

/The Adyanpara Falls are natural waterfalls that seem very beautiful and attract a lot of tourists through its scenic beauty. The waterfall is enjoyed more during the rainy season and the water flow is mostly low during the summers. The place attracts a lot of photographers and nature lovers who come to this cool and calm place to relax and enjoy nature.

Kottakkunnu

Kottakkunnu is also known as the Marine Drive of Malappuram. This beautiful hill garden is a perfect place to visit to enjoy the real beauty of nature.

Thirumandhamkunnu Temple

The Thirumandhamkunnu Temple is a historic Hindu temple in Malappuram. The temple is an important pilgrim centre especially for the 11-day long annual fest celebrated in March and April.

Thirunavaya Temple

Another beautiful temple of the city. It is a historical temple that attracts a lot of people and especially pilgrims who storm in the place every year. The temple is surrounded by beautiful trees and plants and the atmosphere here is completely calm and relaxed. Many people come and sit in the gardens to meditate and escape from the hectic life of the city.

Flora Fantasia Amusement Park

Flora Fantasia Amusement Park is a spectacular water park in Malappuram. Offering its visitors with a wide range of fun activities and rides that give you an amazing experience of the place. It is an ideal place to go to with your friends and family and have some great fun in order to make the best out of your trip to this beautiful city in Kerala.

Conolly’s Plot

onolly’s plot is a beautiful spot in the city of Malappuram. This place allows you to dwell in the natural beauty of this planet. The place has a small path that goes through the whole park. Taking early morning walks in this park is the best thing to do in Malappuram. While walking through this park you will witness huge trees, small streams and a beautiful and astonishing view of the town from a hilltop location.

Nedumkayam Rainforest

Another mesmerizing piece of nature. The Nedumkayam Rainforest also attracts a lot of tourists through its lovely walk within the forest. The major attraction of the place is fresh river water, that has a variety of fish in it. The water is so clean then you can easily see the fish swimming.

Boating

Wondering what to do in Malappuram? Worry not, as the city offers a plethora of activities to indulge in and one of them is boating in the waters of Kerala. As it is one of the popular things to do in Malappuram, no tourist leaves out this activity on their vacation.
