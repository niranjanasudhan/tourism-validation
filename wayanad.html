Wayanad

Known for its soothing temperature all around the year, Wayanad is the ultimate destination to escape the scorching sun of North India. This place doesn’t only offer its cool temperature but along with that, it has untouched woods, misty mountains, and long spread plantations. Often called the spice garden of India, Wayanad is laid with many tourist places and vast orchards of various spices like cardamom, Black pepper, Star Anise, Fenugreek, Black cumin and more. These green orchards of distinct spices give this place a different fragrance which is further enriched with the aroma of coffee plantation, nutrition of organic fruits and vegetables and waving paddy fields. Having said this much about the greenery in the city, it is understood that it will have numerous wildlife sanctuaries, reserves and national parks protecting the rural life of the forests. Adding to all the natural beauty you have India’s largest earth dam and Asia’s second-largest earth dam, Banasura Sagar Dam in Wayanad. Having mighty mountains, dense woods, and gushing rivers, Wayanad is a hub for trekking, hiking, mountaineering, rock climbing, rappelling, camping, and many more adventurous activities. You can trek to India’s largest Earth Dam, Banasura Dam or trek to witness the sun setting behind the mountains from Wayanad’s highest peak, Chembra Peak. The list doesn’t end here, you have the chance to explore potentially Neolithic age at Edakkal Caves. You will find some petroglyphs- prehistoric rock carvings used as a communication tool. Moreover, you will find some amazing examples of ancient architecture and religious beliefs through the monuments like Thirunelli Temple, Karinthandan Temple, Ancient Jain Temple and much more. All these threads weave into an amazing tapestry that will glorify your photo album as well as the album of your life.

Places to Visit in Wayanad

Chembra Peak

Do you have a dream to touch the clouds? If the answer is yes then Chembra Peak is a sure-shot addition to the travel handbook. A paradise for trekking enthusiasts, the beauty of this locale cannot be scribbled in words.

Meenmutty Waterfalls

This waterfall is one of the gems hidden in dense forests of Wayanad. Meenmutty waterfalls are famed as the largest in the district of Wayanad. The water from the falls crashes onto the ground from a height of 1000 ft above sea level in three tiers. Due to its guarded location deep in the woods, there is a hardcore trek of 2 kms involved to reach this treasure.

Kuruva Dweep Islands

Kuruva Dweep is another stunning cluster of islands in the delta of the Kabini river. There is a thick cover of flora and fauna on the island along with the serene ambiance that engulfs this nature’s paradise.

Phantom Rock, Ambalavayal

It is an archaeological wonder that is hidden deep in the dense forests of Wayanad. Cheengeri Mala or Phantom rock is as unique in its appearance as its name. The rock formation resembles a giant skull that has been cast naturally on a hillock.

Thirunelli Temple

The temple is surrounded by three peaks Kambamala, Karimal, Varadoga peak from all around and is dedicated to ”Lord Vishnu”. The shrine has 30 granite pillars that secure it from damage and a stream called Papanasini which flows beside it. According to the popular myth, the stream has the power to dispel all sins and also houses a rock called Pinnappara where prayers are offered to the souls of the departed.

Kanthanpara Falls, Meppadi

Yet another of the masterpieces of nature that is tucked away in the leafy wilderness. Kanthanpara falls is decked by the beauty of bamboo forests from all around, and attracts lots of visitors. With a total height of 30 m, water from the falls cascades down swiftly and takes a deep plunge into the pool below.
